American Sweetgum

Scientific Name:

Liquidambar Styraciflua

Fun Facts:

Sweetgum, also known as American Storax, Hazel-Pine, Bilsted, Redgum, Satin-Walnut, Star-Leaved Gum, or Alligatorwood is a deciduous tree in the genus Liquidambar.

Sweetgum is native to warm temperate areas of eastern North America and tropical montane regions of Mexico and Central America.

Sweetgum is one of the main valuable forest trees in the southeastern United States, and is a popular ornamental tree in temperate climates.

Sweetgum is identifiable by its five-pointed star-shaped leaves (similar to maple leaves) and its hard, spiked fruits.

Sweetgum is currently classified in the plant family Altingiaceae, but was formerly considered a member of the Hamamelidaceae.
